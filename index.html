About the Assessment

1. Overview

Professional Values

Professional Values speak to a student’s motivations, abilities and personality traits within their role at your organization. Each professional value describes different types of challenges and opportunities in which a student is likely to perform well.

2. Characteristics

Key Strengths

Key Strengths refer to students’ unique style and pattern of behavior. Students should capitalize on these Key Strengths to increase effectiveness.

Caution Areas

Caution Areas refer to students’ natural outgrowths of strengths and tendencies that need to be worked on to increase the effectiveness of the individual.

3. Roles & Companies

Model Roles

Model Roles refer to inclusive roles that motivate students.

Job Functions

Job Functions refer to areas of work in which your students excel.

Cultural Preferences

Cultural Preferences are organizational cultures in which students are most able to flourish.

4. Personal Growth

Leadership Anchors

Leadership Anchors serve as a pathfinder for students’ satisfaction, joy, and ongoing success.

Success Keys

Success Keys are typical areas that can prevent students from unlocking their full potential.
